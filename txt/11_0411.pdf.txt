FINAL DECISION AND ORDER

ISSUE:

Whether the Board’s evaluations of the Student are appropriate.

PROCEDURAL HISTORY/DISCUSSION:

This request for hearing was received by the Parents on April 20, 201 1 and a prehearing
conference convened on May 18, 201 l a date requested by the panies. At the request of the
Board’s attorney, the mailing date was extended to allow for sufﬁcient time to attempt to settle
the matter through mediation, and the hearing date of June 24, 2011 was scheduled. On June 16,
201 1 the Board’s attorney submitted asecond request for extension of the mailing date and
postponement of the J une 24, 201 1 hearing date, which was denied. As is set forth in Regs.
Conn. Agencies §10-76h-9, only one extension of the mailing date of the decision may be
granted for continued settlement discussions, and the hearing ofﬁcer shall not have authority to
grant any further postponements or extensions for continued settlement discussions.

Subsequently, the Board’s attorney submitted notiﬁcation that the Board withdraws its
complaint. Therefore, this matter is dismissed, without prejudice.

FINAL DECISION AND ORDER:

The matter is DISMISSED, without prejudice.
