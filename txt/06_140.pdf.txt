Strong, Hearing Officer   FINAL DECISION AND ORDER  PROCEDURAL HISTORY   The Parents (Mother) filed a due process hearing request with the Board of Education on May 30, 2006.  The Board forwarded a copy to the State Department of Education (SDE) 
on that date.  This Hearing Officer was assigned to the case on May 31.  On June 6 a notice of a prehearing conference scheduled on June 13 was sent to the parties.  On June 13 the Hearing Officer placed several telephone calls to the parties.  The Mother did not answer her telephone and a message was left 
regarding the prehearing conference.  After three calls to Mrs. Pavia, the Hearing Officer was advised that the parties had reached a 
settlement at the resolution meeting.  A copy of
 the agreement was sent to the SDE, but 
not to the Hearing Officer.  Mrs. Pavia faxed a copy to the Hearing Officer later that day.  
The Mother telephoned and left a message th
at she thought Mrs. Pavia had notified the Hearing Officer of the settlement.  In the ag
reement dated June 8, the Parents agreed to 
withdraw their due process request.  On June 14 the SDE sent the Hearing Officer a notice to the same effect.  The decision deadline is August 14, 2006.  
 FINAL DECISION AND ORDER  It is ordered that this case be dismissed.
