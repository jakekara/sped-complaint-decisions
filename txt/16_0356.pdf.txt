FINAL DECISION AND ORDER

ISSUES:

1. Did the Board of Education offer the Student a free appropriate public education for the
2015/2016 School Year?

2. If not, is the Easton Country Day School appropriate?

3. If the Board of Education did not offer the Student a free appropriate public education and
the Easton Country Day School is appropriate, is the Student entitled to reimbursement of the
expenses of the Easton Country Day School placement, including speech and 3001211 skills
services and/or placement at Easton Countiy School?

4. If the Board of Education did not offer the Student a free appropriate public education but
the Easton Country Day School is not appropriate, is the Student entitled to compensatory
education services?

PROCEDURAL HISTORY:

The Student requested a special education due process hearing in the above—captioned matter
on February 18, 2016. This Impartial Hearing Ofﬁcer was appointed to hear the case on

F ebruary 23, 2016. A telephonic pre—hearing conference was convened on April 5, 2016.
Attorney Jennifer Laviano appeared on behalf of the Student and Attorney Marsha Moses
appeared on behalf of the Board of Education. The deadline for ﬁling the ﬁnal decision was
established to be May 3, 2016. An evidentiary hearing was scheduled for June 17, 2016.

On April 11, 2016 the Student submitted a written request for a thirty—day postponement and
extension of the timelines to conduct the hearing and to ﬁle the ﬁnal decision in this case to
June 2, 2016. The puipose of the requested postponement and extension was to allow the
parties an opportunity to mediate. The Board of Education agreed to the requested
postponement and it was granted.

On May 30, 2016, the Student reported that the parties’ mediation was scheduled for June 1,
2016 and submitted a second written request for a thirty-day postponement and extension of
the timelines to conduct the hearing and to file the ﬁnal decision in this case to July 1, 2016.
The purpose of the requested postponement and extension was to allow the parties an
opportunity to pursue due process. The Board of Education did not obj ect to the requested
postponement and extension and it was granted.

On June 1, 2016, the Student reported that the parties’ mediation was successful and that the
parties reached a tentative agreement for resolution of the case. The Student requested that
the hearing scheduled for June 17, 2016 be postponed and that the timelines to conduct the
hearing and to ﬁle the ﬁnal decision in this be extended for an additional 30 days to allow the
parties to pursue due process should the settlement fail. The Board of Education joined in the
request and it was granted.

On July 20, 2016, the Student reported that the parties had reached a resolution of the dispute
and requested that the matter be dismissed with prejudice.

FINAL DECISION AND ORDER:

It is ordered that the Student’s request for dismissal is granted and this matter is dismissed
with prejudice.
