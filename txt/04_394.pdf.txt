Hearing Officer   FINAL DECISION AND ORDER  SUMMARY:  This matter was assigned on December 17, and a prehearing conference was held 
on December 23.  On December 23, the attorney for the Parents submitted a request for 
an extension of the mailing date of the decision so that the parties could attempt to settle 
the matter through mediation.  That request was granted in accordance with Section 10-

76h-9(e) of the Connecticut Regulations which provides for a 30-day postponement for 
settlement discussions between the parties.  The first hearing date was scheduled for 
February 10.  On January 31, the Board&#8482;s attorney submitted a request for postponement of the 
hearing so that the mediation could take place on the scheduled hearing date of February 
10.  The request was denied in accordance with the regulations, which provides that the hearing officer shall not have authority to grant any further postponements or extensions.  

On February 3, the attorney for the Parents submitted a notice that the parties were not 
prepared to go forward with the scheduled hearing date.  In accordance with Section 10-76h-9(e) of the Connecticut Regulations, if the parties are not prepared to go forward 
with the hearing, the hearing officer shall dismiss the hearing request without prejudice. 
 FINAL DECISION AND ORDER:  The matter is 
DISMISSED, without prejudice.
