 Elizabeth Oppenheim, Hearing Officer FINAL DECISION AND ORDER 
ISSUE: Whether the Board&#8482;s neuropsychological ev
aluation of the Student is appropriate. PROCEDURAL HISTORY/DISCUSSION: 
The Board filed this request for hearing, which was received by the Parents on January 20, 2010.  A prehearing conference was held on February 5, 2010. 
The initial hearing dates that were scheduled were postponed at the request of the parties so that a mediation and settlement discussions could proceed. 

On April 29, 2010, the Parents&#8482; attorney submitted a 
motion to consolidate all 4 cases involving the 
Student and her twin sibling. In 
accordance with Regs. of Conn. State Agencies Sec. 10-76h-8(f)(3) the hearing officers involved in all four matters cons
ulted.  The Motion was granted in part on April 30, 2010 and the two cases involving the Student in this case were consolidated.   

A prehearing conference was held on the matter 
of Student v. Willington, Case No. 10-0387, and the 
matter proceeded to hearing on May 6, 2010. 

At the May 6, 2010 hearing, counsel for the Board and the Parents jointly requested that this case [Case No. 10-0286] be dismissed without prejudice, and 
requested that the Case No. 10-0387, Student v. Willington, proceed to hearing as the sole case at 
this time.  Case No. 10-0286, Willington Board of 
Education v. Student, was dismissed at the hearing. 
FINAL DECISION AND ORDER: 
The matter is 
DISMISSED, without prejudice
