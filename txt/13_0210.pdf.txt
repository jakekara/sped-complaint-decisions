F INAL DECISION AND ORDER
ISSUES:

1. Did the Board provide the Student a free, appropriate, pubhc education (F APE) in the
least restrictive environment (LRE) during the 2010—201 1 school year, including
summer session?

2. Did the Board provide the Student at FAPE in the LRE during the 201 1-2012 school
year, including summer session?

3, a. Did the Board propose an educational placement that would provide the Student a

FAPE in the LRE for the 2012—2013 school year?
b. If not, was the Parents’ unilateral placement appropriate?

SUMMARY:

A special education due process hearing was requested by the Parent’s attorney and received by
the Board on November 13, 2012. Attorney Laviano appeared on behalf of the Parent and
Attorney Laubin on behalf Of the Board during a telephonic pre-hearing conference on December
1 1, 2012. During the pre—hearing conference, Attorney Laviano requested a 30-day extension, to
which Attorney Laubin agreed. The motion for extension was granted and the deadline for the
ﬁnal decision and order was extended to February 28, 2013. On February 16, 2013, pursuant to
a settlement agreement between the palties, Attorney Laviano withdrew the request for a due

process hearing.
FINAL DECISION AND ORDER:

The matter is DISMISSED with prejudice.
