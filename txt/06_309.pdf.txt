Hearing Officer       FINAL DECISION  
 The prehearing in this matter was conducte
d on October 31, 2006 and the Board who was not represented by counsel at the time, acknowledged they had received the notice of prehearing but was unavailable to participate nonetheless.  Issues included reimbursement for a wilderness program, 
all IEPs from April 13, 2005 forward and 
procedural violations.  Heari
ng dates were set and the Student made it clear that they 
were requesting that the hearing be comple
ted within 75 days.  Subsequent to the prehearing conference counsel appeared for the Board and requested a continuance of some of the hearing dates.  The Student di
d not agree and the request was denied.  The Student filed a Motion for Production which was 
granted.  At the first day of hearing the parties indicated they were close to a settle
ment and subsequently reported that the matter 
had been resolved and they were asking that it be dismisse
d with prejudice.   FINAL DECISION AND ORDER:
  The hearing is dismissed WITH PREJUDICE.
