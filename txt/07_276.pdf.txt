ISSUES 
 
 1. Should the Student have been exite
d from special education services by 
the Planning and Placement team ("PPT
") on June 19, effective August 31, 2007, or continued receiving special education services from the Board? 
 PROCEDURAL HISTORY  
 The Student's attorney requested this 
hearing on August 31 by faxing a complaint 
form and letter to the Bristol Board of E
ducation's Director of Special Services 
("Director") and to the State Department of Education ("SD
E").  This Hearing Officer was assigned to the case on September 5.  On September 14, Atty. Yoder filed an 
appearance on behalf of the Board.  A prehearing conference was held on September 20.  
The decision deadline was set at November 
14, 2007.  The Student's attorney advised the 
Hearing Officer that a PPT meeting was held 
on the previous day and that she would be withdrawing his complaint.  No hearing da
te was scheduled.  On September 24, Atty. 
Zanger wrote to the Hearing Officer stating that that the Board had agreed to continue to program for the Student and planned a program, 
subject to adjustment, in Bristol.  She 
asked that the case be dismissed without prejudice. 

 FINAL DECISION AND ORDER 
 
It is ordered that this case shall be dismissed without prejudice.
