FINAL DECISION AND ORDER

ISSUES:

1. Did the Board of Education provide the Student a free appropriate education for the
Student for the 2013/2014 and 2014/2015 School Years?

2. If not, is the Student entitled to compensatory education and if so, what would

characterize such compensatory education?
3. ls the Board of Education’s proposed program for the 2015/2016 School Year

appropriate?

4. If not, is the Winchendon School appropriate?

5. 1f the Winchendon School is appropriate, is the Student entitled to reimbursement of
expenses for, or placement at, Winchendon School?

PROCEDURAL HISTORY:

The Student requested a special education due process hearing in the above-captioned matter
on July 14, 2015. The lmpaltial Hearing Ofﬁcer was appointed to hear the case on J uly 14,
2015. A telephonic preheating conference was held on July 21, 2015. Attorney Christopher
Tracey appeared on behalf of the Board of Education and Attorney Lawrence Berliner
appeared on behalf of the Student. At that time, evidentiaiy hearings were scheduled for
August 27, 2015 and August 28, 2015.

On J uly 24, 2015 the Student reported that the dispute had been ﬁnally resolved, and

It is ordered that the Student’s request for dismissal is granted and this matter is dismissed.
