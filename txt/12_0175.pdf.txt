FINAL DECISION AND ORDER

ISSUES:

1, Was the program offered by the Board from December 7, 2010 to the end of the 2010-201 1 school year
appropriate and did it provide the Student with a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) in the least
restrictive environment (LRE)?

2. Is the program offered by the Board for the 2011—2012 school year appropriate and does it provide the
Student with FAPE in the LRE? If not;

3. Does the Student require a private placement that provides speciﬁc suppoxts for the Student’s multiple
disabilities of autism and visual impairment?

4. Is the Student entitled to compensatory education for the denial of FAPB from December 7, 2010 to the end
of the 2010-2011 school year?

SUMMARY and PROCEDURAL HISTORY:

The Student has been identiﬁed with Autism and is entitled to receive FAPE as deﬁned in the Individuals with
Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEA) 20 U.S.C. §1401 et seq. and Connecticut General Statute §10—
76a. At a PPT meeting, the Parent rejected the program offered by the Board for the 2011~ 2012 school year.
The Parent requested a private placement that provided speciﬁc suppons for the Student's multiple needs of
autism and visual impairment, The Board refused the Parent’s request.

On November 22, 2011, the Board received notice of the Parent’s request for due process, The parties agreed to
go to a resolution meeting which was scheduled for November 23, 2011. An impartial hearing ofﬁcer was
appointed on November 22, 201 1 and a preheating conference was held on December 1, 2011. A hearing date
of January 30, 2012 was chosen by the parties. In an electronic transmission, the Parent's attorney advised the
hearing ofﬁcer that the parties were able to resolve the matter and requested a Withdrawal without prejudice.
The date for mailing the Final Order and Decision is February 5, 2012.

FlNAL DECISION AND ORDER:

THE MATTER IS DISMISSED.
