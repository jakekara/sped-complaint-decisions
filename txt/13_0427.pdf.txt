FINAL DECISION AND ORDER

ISSUES:

1. Did the Haitford Public Schools (“Board”) deny the Student a free and
appropriate public education (“FAPE”) for the time periods April 29, 201 1~
2012; 2012-2013?

2. Did the Board commit procedural Violations during the time periods stated in
Issue No. 1 sufﬁcient to cause the Student a denial of a FAPE?

3. If the answer to Issues 1 or 2 is “Yes”; what is the appropriate remedy?

4. Does the Student require Extended Year Services?

PROCEDURAL HISTORY:

The complaint was ﬁled on April 22, 2013. The Parties engaged in a resolution
meeting on April 29, 2013. A pre-hearing conference was held on April 30, 2013, where
the issues listed above were identiﬁed. Initial due process hearings were set for June 12
and June 13, 2013. The Panies agreed to engage in mediation and subsequently
requested a postponement of the hearing dates in an effort to reach ﬁnal resolution. On
June 24, 2013, Counsel for the Parents ﬁled a written withdrawal, with prejudice, of their
request for a due process hearing.

FINAL DECISION AND ORDER:

Through their Counsel, the Parents have ﬁled a written withdrawal, with prejudice, of
their request for a due process hearing. With no ﬁnther issues remaining for hearing, this
matter is DISMISSED WITH PREIUDICE.
