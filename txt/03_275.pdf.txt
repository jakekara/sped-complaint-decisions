Hearing Officer   FINAL DECISION AND ORDER  PROCEDURAL HISTORY:  The student is a seven year old child who has been diagnosed as autistic and has been determined eligible for special education and related services as defined in 20 U.S.C. &#167;&#167; 
1401 et esq.  The parents requested a due process hearing on August 28, 2003 and a pre-hearing conference was conducted on September 10, 2003.  At the pre-hearing 
conference hearing dates were set for November 3 & 5, 2003.  Additional hearing dates 
were required and set for December 8, 9, 22, 2003, January 14, February 6 & 11, 2004. 
 The parents filed a Motion for Telephonic Testimony, which was timely opposed by the 

Board.  On or about December 3, 2003 the Motion was granted with six conditions, 
which needed to be complied with in order for testimony to be taken by telephone. 
The parents and the Board on the February 11, 2003 hearing date informed the hearing 
officer that an agreement had been reached by the parties and no further testimony was 
required.  The parties were given time to reduce the agreement to writing and to execute 
the agreement. 
  The parents&#8482; attorney informed the hearing officer by mail that the agreement had been 

executed and that the matter was settled.  The date for the mailing of the Final Decision 
and order is May 3, 2004.  FINAL DECISION AND ORDER:  The matter is 
DISMISSED, with prejudice.
