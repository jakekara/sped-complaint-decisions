  ISSUES   1. Whether the student was denied
 a FAPE for the 2005-2006 school year 
due to the Board's failure to (a) timely iden
tify the student and hold a timely PPT after the 
student was diagnosed with Autism, (b) prope
rly and timely evaluate the student in all 
areas of suspected disability, (c) offer an appropriate program with a high enough level of 
intensity or structure, including related, extended school day, and extended school year services, as well as sufficient hours, to
 remediate the student's disabilities; 
 2. Whether the student was denied
 a FAPE for the 2006-2007 school year 
due to the Board's  failure to (a) offer an 
appropriate program with
 a high enough level of intensity or structure, including related, extended school day, and extended school year services, (b) offer or provide sufficient hours, to remediate the st
udent's disabilities; 
 3. Whether the student should be placed
 at the Connecticut Center for Child 
Development; 
 4. Whether the Board should reimburse the parents for all expenses, 

including costs for materials and equi
pment for the 2005-2006; and 2006-2007 school 
years;  5. Whether the Board should provide compensatory services. 
  SUMMARY   The hearing was requested on March 29, 2007;  the deadline for the final decision and order was June 12, 2007.  A prehearing 
conference convened on April 12, 2007.  On May 24, 2007 the Parties advised the Hearing Officer that a settlement had been reached 
whereby the Request for Hearing was withdrawn.
