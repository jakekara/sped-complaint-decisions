ISSUES: 1. Whether the Student is eligible for Special Education and related services; 
2. Whether the Board should reimburse the Pare
nts for an Independent Education Evaluation; 3. Whether the Board failed to identif
y the Student pursuant to Child Find; 4. Whether the Board provided the Parents with the Procedural Safeguards; 5. Whether the Board failed to offer FAPE to the Student; 6. Whether the Board failed to convene a PPT; 7. Whether the Student should be placed at a private school; 8. Whether the Board should reimburse the Pare
nts for the private school since February 2009; 9. Whether the Student is entitled to compensatory education. 
10. Whether any of the above issues are limited by the two year statute of limitations. 
SUMMARY: On August 2, 2010, the Board received the Request for Due Process.  The Prehearing Conference was held on August 25, 2010 and hearing dates were scheduled. On September 14, 2010, Counsel for the Parent notified the 
Hearing Officer that the parties had reached a settleme
nt agreement, withdrew the Due Process Request, and 
requested that the matter be dismissed with prejudice. 
FINAL DECISION AND ORDER: 
The matter is DISMISSED with prejudice.
