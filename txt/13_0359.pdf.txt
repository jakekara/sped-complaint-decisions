FINAL DECISION AND ORDER

ISSUES:

1. Was the Board’s psychiatric evaluation appropriate?
2. If not, should the Board be required to fund the second psychiatric evaluation

requested by the parent?

PROCEDURAL HISTORY/ SUMMARY:

The Board ﬁled a Request for Due Process Hearing on March 19, 2013 to defend its
psychiatric evaluation. The Parent had requested that the Board pay for a second
evaluation at a Planning and Placement Team meeting. The Hearing Ofﬁcer conducted a
telephonic Prehearing Conference on April 2, 2013. A hearing date was scheduled for
April 26, 2013. On April 16, 2013, the Parent notiﬁed the Hearing Ofﬁcer that he was
withdrawing his request for the Board’s payment of a second evaluation. Subsequently,
the Board’s attorney informed the Hearing Ofﬁcer that the Board would be withdrawing
the Request for Due Process Hearing without prejudice.

FINAL DECISION AND ORDER:

The matter is DISMISSED without prejudice.
